V. FIRE/LIFE SAFETY TRAINING OUTLINE

Safety is the primary concern in the residence halls and to have a good overall safety program, a dedicated effort is needed from all parties involved. It is essential that each resident receive training in fire prevention and know the proper procedures should a fire occur. This training outline corresponds with the Fire/Life Safety Regulations and Policies and the self-inspection form.

I. FIRE PREVENTION

    A. SMALL APPLIANCE
    • Halogen lamps are not permitted.
    • Space heaters are prohibited.
    • Hair dryers are not clothes dryers.
    • Be sure other appliances such as clothes irons, electric rollers, curling irons are turned OFF before leaving the room.
    • Refer to Section III-J for Appliance Policy.
    B. HOUSEKEEPING
    • Empty trash -- do not place waste cans in the corridor.
    C. SMOKING
    • Use proper size ash trays.
    • Do not dispose of cigarette butts in waste baskets.
    • DO NOT smoke in bed.
    D. DECORATIONS
    • ALL decorations must be U.L. listed flame retardent.
    • Encourage students not to over decorate their room.
    • Refer to Section III-L for Decoration Policy
    E. LIGHTING
    • DO NOT obstruct lighting units, especially emergency lighting units and exit signs.
    F. CANDLES
    • NOT permitted.
    G. ELECTRICAL
    • DO NOT overload.
    • Refer to Section III-H for Electrical Policy.
    H. STAIRWELL/FIRE DOORS
    • Are a required means of egress. DO NOT block open.
    I. COOKING
    • NOT PERMITTED in rooms.
    • Refer to Section III-J for Cooking Policy.

II. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

    A. EVACUATION PROCEDURES
    • Emphasize evacuation plan for the building.
    • Discuss information on the posted plan.
    B. HANDICAP STUDENTS
    • Request volunteer assistance.
    • Relocate to stairwells.
    • Report location to firemen/University of Kentucky Police.
    C. FIRE ALARM ACTIVATION
    • Pull stations - know locations.
    • Smoke detectors - never smoke beneath a detector.
    • Sprinkler systems - Activates fire alarm.
    • Annunicator panels - Indicates location of alarm.
    D. EMERGENCY NUMBER
    • 911 - When dialed contacts U.K. Police Officer.
    • Use for Emergencies only.
    E. FALSE ALARMS
    • Report anyone abusing the fire alarm to the Hall Director.

III. FIRE SUPPRESSION EQUIPMENT

    A. Fire Extinguishers
    • All Residence Halls have "ABC-All Purpose" type fire extinguishers that can be used on any type fire.
    B. Report all vandalism of units or discharged units to the University Fire Marshal (257-6326).

IV. GENERAL SAFETY

    A. Report Unsafe Conditions to the office of Occupational Safety and Health (257-3827).
    B. Bicycles - Not permitted in buildings - Refer to Section III-K for information.
    C. Report all injuries to the office of Occupational Safety and Health. The injury report form
    (Form 6) can be found here.
