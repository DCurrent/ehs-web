Procedure for Conducting a Wipe Test

Equipment

  • survey map
  • filter paper
  • scintillation fluid
  • scintillation vials
  • a scintillation counter calibrated in units of dpm to detect the contaminants that may be found in the laboratory
  • laboratory coat and gloves

Procedure

  • determine locations that you will be wipe testing*
  • note areas on the survey map
  • using a moistened piece of filter paper wipe approximately 100 square centimeters of the surface
  • repeat this process for all locations listed on the map
  • place each filter paper in a scintillation vial, add scintillation fluid and cap
  • place scintillation vials in scintillation counter for analysis according to the units standard operating procedures.
  • place printout of results in "Radioactive Wipe Test Survey Results" binder for review by Radiation Safety Staff
* Locations to check for contamination
  • door handle to the laboratory on refrigerator/freezer
  • bench tops and desks where contamination should not be found
  • floors adjacent to benches where material is used
  • fume hood sill
  • potentially contaminated equipment
  • liquid scintillation counter or gamma counter
  • any item or location in the laboratory that should not be contaminated
