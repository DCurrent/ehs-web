On-line Sources of Hazard Information

  • Laboratory Chemical Safety Summaries for 88 chemicals commonly encountered in labs
    (hard copies of these LCSSs are in Prudent Practices, pp. 235-413)

  • Where to Find MSDSs on Internet, includes Cornell MSDS site with 250,000 MSDSs and Vermont SIRI (University of Vermont) with 180,000

  • NIOSH Pocket Guide to Chemical Hazards, on line version, quick reference to nearly 700 chemicals

  • National Library of Medicine--GENE-TOX, Hazardous Substances Data Bank (HSDB), TOXLINE, Environmental Mutagen Information Center and several others

  • National Toxicology Program, 10th Report on Carcinogens, 2002

  • Chemical Health and Safety Data, from the National Toxicology Program

  • Emergency Response Guidebook, A Guidebook for First Responders During the Initial Stage of Dangerous Goods/Hazardous Materials Incident, on line version

  • International Chemical Safety Cards, US version by NIOSH

  • Hazardous Substance Fact Sheets from the New Jersey Right to Know program, plain English descriptions of common household, workplace and environmental chemicals

  • MSDSs for infectious microorganisms Health Canada's Material Safety Data Sheets

  • OSHA PELs 1910.1000 Table Z-1, Limits for Air Contaminants

  • OSHA PELs 1910.1000 Table Z-2, Toxic and Hazardous Substances

  • Chemical Backgrounders, from the National Safety Council, good references and links

  • Incompatible Chemicals, list developed by the Department of Animal Sciences, University of Kentucky College of Agriculture
